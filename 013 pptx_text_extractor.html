PPTX Animation Extractor Guide
Purpose

This tool reads a .pptx file and pulls out every piece of text — in the exact order the animations play during your slideshow.

Instead of clicking through your whole presentation to note what appears when, you get a clean numbered list: line #1 appears first, #2 second, and so on — per slide.

Static text (shapes with no animation) is listed separately at the bottom of each slide.

Nothing is uploaded. Everything runs inside your browser. Your file never leaves your device.

How to use
1
Drop your .pptx file onto the upload area, or click it to browse
2
The tool processes every slide automatically — no settings needed
3
Each slide shows its text in animation playback order, numbered #1, #2…
4
Download as TXT (readable, slide-by-slide) or CSV (opens in Excel)
Reading the results
anim
Animated text — has a number (#1, #2…) showing when it appears during the slideshow. Sorted by animation sequence within each slide.
static
Static text — visible on the slide from the start, not animated. Listed after animated items with a dash (—) instead of a number.
Good to know

Per-paragraph detection: If a text box has 10 bullet points each animated separately, you'll see 10 numbered entries — not just 1 for the whole box.

Character range aware: PowerPoint stores animations as character ranges. This tool maps those ranges back to individual paragraphs accurately.

.pptx only — not .ppt (old format) or .pptm. If you have a .pptm, rename it to .pptx first — the text extraction still works.
